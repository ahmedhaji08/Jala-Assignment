Hyderabad, located in southern India, is a vibrant metropolis and the capital city of Telangana state, also serving as the joint capital of Andhra Pradesh. It is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, historical monuments, and booming IT industry.

Founded in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, Hyderabad has a unique blend of the old and the new. The city’s iconic landmark, the Charminar, stands as a symbol of its glorious past, surrounded by bustling markets like Laad Bazaar famous for bangles, pearls, and traditional jewelry. Hyderabad is often called the "City of Pearls" due to its historical role as a center for pearl trade.

The city’s cuisine is equally famous, especially the Hyderabadi Biryani, a fragrant and flavorful rice dish that draws food lovers from all over the world. Besides its cultural richness, Hyderabad is a major IT and biotech hub, earning the nickname "Cyberabad" because of its sprawling technology parks such as HITEC City and Genome Valley.

Hyderabad also prides itself on its educational institutions, historical sites like the Golconda Fort and Qutb Shahi Tombs, and its warm, cosmopolitan population. The city beautifully balances tradition and modernity, making it a fascinating place to visit or live.
